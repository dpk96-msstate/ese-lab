Research

Our research pushes the boundaries of how software requirements are conceived, validated, and integrated with testing and security practices.

Empirical Requirements Engineering & Validation

Investigating how software requirements are elicited, refined, and validated in practice through empirical studies. Our work examines how requirements quality, creativity, and structure influence downstream activities such as testing, maintenance, and security assurance.

Key Research Areas:

  • Requirements Quality Assessment: Empirical evaluation of ambiguity, completeness, and testability in functional and non-functional requirements
  • Creative Requirements Practices: Studying methods that encourage innovation and creativity while maintaining rigor and traceability
  • Requirements Validation Techniques: Systematic approaches for validating requirements using reviews, prototypes, and executable artifacts
  • Empirical Study Design: Controlled experiments, case studies, and surveys to understand real-world requirements engineering practices

Impact & Applications:

This research informs evidence-based guidance for practitioners and educators, leading to more reliable requirements and improved alignment between stakeholder intent and implemented systems.

Integration of Requirements and Software Testing

Developing empirically grounded methods that strengthen the relationship between requirements and testing. We study how requirements artifacts can drive effective test design and how test results can be used to assess and improve requirements quality.

Technical Innovations:

  • Requirements-Based Test Design: Techniques for systematically deriving unit, integration, and system tests from requirements
  • Testability of Requirements: Empirical analysis of how requirements characteristics affect test effectiveness and fault detection
  • Bidirectional Traceability: Methods for maintaining and validating trace links between requirements and test artifacts
  • Tool Support: Lightweight tools that support automated analysis and validation of requirements–test alignment

Research Outcomes:

Our studies provide empirical evidence on how tighter requirements–testing integration improves defect detection, regression resilience, and long-term software maintainability.

Creativity and Human Factors in Software Engineering

Exploring the role of creativity, cognition, and human decision-making in requirements engineering and software development. We empirically study how individuals and teams generate, evaluate, and refine innovative requirements.

Core Research Focus:

  • Creativity Evaluation Frameworks: Empirical models and metrics for assessing creativity in requirements artifacts
  • Human-Centered RE Methods: Studying how stakeholders reason about, negotiate, and evolve requirements
  • Educational Interventions: Evaluating pedagogical techniques for teaching creative and effective requirements engineering
  • Decision-Making Analysis: Investigating cognitive biases and their impact on requirements and testing outcomes

Methodological Contributions:

This work advances the scientific understanding of creativity in software engineering and provides evidence-based practices for improving both industrial and educational outcomes.

Software Security Requirements & Empirical Assurance

Studying how security requirements are specified, validated, and tested in real-world systems. Our research emphasizes empirical evaluation of security-related requirements and their systematic integration into software testing processes.

Security-Focused Research Areas:

  • Security Requirements Elicitation: Empirical analysis of practices for identifying and documenting security needs
  • Test-Driven Security Validation: Using security-focused tests to assess the adequacy of security requirements
  • Requirements–Security Alignment: Studying mismatches between stated security requirements and implemented safeguards
  • Evidence-Based Security Assurance: Leveraging empirical data to support claims about software security

Industry Relevance:

Our findings help organizations build more secure systems by grounding security decisions in empirical evidence and systematic validation rather than ad hoc practices.

Research Impact

Our research advances the state of empirical software engineering by providing evidence-based methods that improve requirements quality, testing effectiveness, and software security. Through collaborations with academia and industry, our work informs practice, supports education, and contributes to the development of reliable, creative, and secure software systems.

About Us

The Software Engineering Research and Analysis Lab (SERA) is a research lab focused on advancing knowledge and driving innovation in requirements engineering and its integration with testing and secure software development through empirical, data-driven methods.

Our Mission

Is to develop empirically grounded methods and tools that support creative requirements engineering and strengthen the connections between requirements, testing, and software security.

Our Vision

A future in which software systems are built on well-understood, creative, and secure requirements that are systematically validated through tight integration with testing and empirical evidence.

Our Focus

Is on empirically studying and developing methods that identify, evaluate, and validate creative and secure software requirements through their systematic integration with testing activities.
